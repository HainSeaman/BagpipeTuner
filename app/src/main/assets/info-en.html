Bagpipe Tuner – developed by a piper for pipers

This app is a tuner for the Scottish bagpipes, the Great Highland Bagpipes. It works like a classical tuning device but is specifically designed for pipes. Bagpipes are not tuned in equal temperament like a piano but use pure or adjusted intervals. Therefore, it is very useful to use a tuner specially developed for this purpose – it makes tuning the pipes much easier.
A particular challenge is the extremely high and rather piercing tones of the chanter, which are very rich in overtones. This makes detecting the actual pitch quite difficult, especially for a microphone not designed for it, such as that of a smartphone.
Documented measurements from 1885 showed a pitch of 441 Hz for the chanter’s fundamental tone. In the mid‑1950s, the average value was already about 459 Hz. Today, we are moving toward 480 Hz and even higher.

The app is freeware and open source. It contains no ads, no trackers, and no cookies. It runs entirely on your device, thus ensuring your privacy.

The tuner app automatically detects the tones being played and shows how far they deviate from the desired target pitch.

Features

  • Tuner for chanter and drones
  • The desired fundamental tone can be set (manually or by recording)
  • Various sound types (“New Band”, “Warm”, “Brilliant”, “Old Band”, and “Custom Sound Type”)
  • Adjustable animation speed and recording time
  • Create your own sound type

Main Page

  1. Tuning Scale – Visual display showing the tuning of the currently played tone.
  2. Tuning Indicator – Real‑time display showing whether the note is too high, too low, or just right.
  3. Tuner Display – Shows deviation in cents, note name, and frequency.
  4. Tuner Start – Starts recording for tuning.
  5. Fundamental Tone – Displays the set fundamental pitch.
  6. Tuner Mode – Select whether to tune chanter or drones.
  7. Sound Type – Displays the selected sound type.

Settings

  1. Fundamental Tone – Set via slider.
  2. Display – Shows current fundamental tone.
  3. Record Fundamental Tone – Record the chanter to determine pitch.
  4. Recording Time – Set duration of recording.
  5. Animation Speed – Adjust the needle’s movement speed.
  6. Sound Type – Select the preferred type.

The Sound Type

Over time, pitch and tonal character of pipes have changed. In earlier days, tuning was usually done by ear – today, tuning is based on electronic tuners and standardized scales.
